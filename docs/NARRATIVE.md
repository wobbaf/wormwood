# Narrative — Wormwood

## Setting

A mid-sized city under quiet occupation. Not wartime — the war ended years ago. What's left is administration: permits, curfews, approved suppliers, a guard presence that's always just slightly too visible. The city functions. People get on with their lives. But everyone knows what's allowed and what isn't.

The apothecary district is old. Your grandmother's shop — **Wormwood & Root** — has been there for forty years.

---

## Characters

### Mara (player character)
Granddaughter. Left the city years ago, never planned to come back. Practical, a little guarded. Doesn't know much about plants or remedies — she's learning. That's the player learning too.

### Grandma Elsa
Never seen alive. Present in everything: her handwriting in the margins of remedy books, her arrangement of the greenhouse, the way customers talk about her. Slowly revealed to be far more than a shopkeeper. She was building something. She got too close to something. She's the mystery.

### Voss
Black market ingredient trader. Knew Elsa well. Friendly, unreliable, occasionally dangerous. Knows more than he says. Will share — but always wants something in return.

### Captain Brennan
City guard. Comes in as a customer early on — headaches, he says. Polite. Watches the shop a beat too long when he leaves. Not obviously threatening. That's what makes him threatening.

### Lena
Young woman, comes in every few days. Buys the same tincture each time. Eventually you realize she's not buying it for herself. Who is she buying it for and why can't they come in person?

### Old Petre
Retired forager, occasional supplier. Knew Elsa since before the occupation. Talks in circles. Remembers everything but shares it sideways, in stories that take three visits to understand.

---

## Grandma's Secret

*[Full spoilers — design reference only]*

Elsa was part of a small network helping people disappear — those targeted by the regime for removal. She didn't move people herself. She provided the remedies that made them look dead: slowed pulse, pallor, undetectable without a proper physician. The network would collect them before burial.

She was getting close to moving someone important — someone with knowledge that could expose the regime's administration of the city's water supply (slow poisoning, keeping the population docile).

She was poisoned with her own compound, modified. Someone in the network betrayed her, or someone outside discovered her. That's what Mara has to determine.

The locked greenhouse contains the last batch of the compound, near completion. What Mara does with it is the ending.

## Tone

- Quiet dread, not horror
- Warmth in the shop itself — customers are real people with real problems
- The occupation is mundane evil, not cartoonish
- Grief as subtext throughout — Mara loved her grandmother more than she admitted
- Occasional dark humor (Voss provides most of it)
